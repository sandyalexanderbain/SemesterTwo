Concise History.

Ting Kok Tsuen (TKT), known as 汀角村 in Chinese characters, is pronounced as Ding1 gok3 Chuen1 in Cantonese, a dialect of Mandarin. The roots of this village trace back to before 1688 during the Qing Dynasty's Kangxi era. Historical records have provided evidence that the original name of TKT was Ting Kai and was a multi-surname settlement of 14 differnet clans. TKT was part of an alliance formed with nearby villages dominated by the dominant ethnic group, Hakka, in order to create mutual connections and protection amongst the different villages. One of the villages that was also in this alliance was Tai Mei Tuk (TMT), as mentioned in the visitor guide of this fabulous guide.

In the Early 20th century, evidence uncovered in 1911 uncovered TKT's population to be 669 people total with 301 males and 368 females. As the decades passed, TKT continues to adopt a conversation approach as urbanisation continues to increase. In 1985, the wetlands and parts of the Pat Sin Leng country park that TKT intergrated into are publicly declared as sites of special scientific interest for unique mangroves. To this day, there is still ecological value possessed within TKT, rare flora such as the Lumnitzera racemosa give rise to the preserved value.

TKT's history is a strong representation of how traditional culture, cultural preservation and environmental stewardship can be honed together whilst being at present day home to greater than 10x fold the people that once lived in here in TKT.
