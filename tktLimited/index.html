Ting Kok Tsuen (TKT)


TKT, a small local village, part of the Tai Po (TP) District.

🇭🇰 The TP District is 1 of 18 Districts in Hong Kong (abbreviated as HKSAR or HK). 🇭🇰

A local village is a traditional residential area, originally for local indigenous farmers. Still predominantly local indigenous farmers, though expansion and access for locals from highrise areas of HK as well as expats continues to increase. HK has 18 districts in total, all for the purpose of adminstrative and development planning, each district is represented by a distrinct council. Tai Po is 1 of 18.

Main Regions of HK

    ~~~ 3 Main Regions ~~~

  1. New Territories (NT)
    The orange area, mainland, with the "New Territories label".

  2. Kowloon Peninsula (Kln / Kln P.)
    The white area connected to NT and just under the "Hong Kong" label.

  3. Hong Kong Island (HKI)
    The white area, island, with the "Hong Kong Island" label.

The houses that a village is made up of are frequently larger in living space (e.g. square feet or square metres) and low-rise buildings opposed to high-rise and tighter space confinements in centeral areas of Hong Kong. HK island buildings contrast village houses as it is concentrated with economical, political and historical aspects of HK.

    NT Fun Facts!
  1. The NT holds 9 out of the 18 HK districts and is the most abundant main region to find local villages.
  2. The NT makes up 86% of Hong Kong's land with over 4 million residents.
  3. NT is nature dominated, with 40% of the total area being country parks and nature reserves.
