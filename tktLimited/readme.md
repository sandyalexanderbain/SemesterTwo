My hometown website involves simple navigation tailored for those that are also less
technologically involved in their daily lives with three html pages that are linked together and can be jumped 
back and forth to and from with <a> tag link buttons that have a visual change in colour
to indicate interactability.

The layout of my website involves a banner on each of the three html pages with centered
titles and then subsequent text that is justified and centered for an organised and easy
to read format. Additioanlly, images are included and interactable widgets such as an open
street map and image carousel follow a top to bottom order. The top to bottom order
ensures coherent and ease of understanding as to how you digest and discover more
information about my hometown as you spend time on this website.

The colour choices I made were based on subtle and dark colours that can be easy to read,
contrast and are pleasant to the eye as to not create for a chaotic colour scheme which
would negatively affect readability and coherency of the entire website's layout.

The main challenges I faced include understanding how to create a navigation bar and to be
able to be able to implement bootstrap along with my main.css code as intially I had to
adapt to the slight confict that occurred due to some overlapping areas that both at one
point attempted to change.
