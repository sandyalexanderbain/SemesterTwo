Some Things About Me

By 1 in 8,300,000,000.

Hi there, I'm Sandy. I turned 20 years old, nearly 3 weeks ago...

I currently attend the University of Aberdeen for a 4-year degree in computer science. Unexpected, yet appreciative for being able to think about my full-time degree choice.

I am by blood ethnically Han Chinese (99.9% according to a 23AndMe DNA test a few years ago), and adopted by two Scottish parents. My mother grew up in Irvine, near Glasgow, and my father grew up in Doune, closer to Edinburgh.

Born and raised for 16 years in Hong Kong, I have had a fortunate upbringing. At the same time I have experienced some interesting incongruent cultural expectations and normalities.

For my kindergarten and first year of primary school, I attended local schools taught in Cantonese. Cantonese is the dominant language spoken in Hong Kong, a dialect of the parent language Mandarin, Pu Tong Hua.

Being a Chinese looking child with Western parents and being best friends with a ethnically darker skin toned girl led to bullying at the local primary school. Yet this was not the reason I moved schools, to my parents, it was justifiably to do with the daily four plus hours of homework and tutoring I partook in at 6 years old.

Anyhow, moving schools served to be a challenge as the bullying nudged me to become shy, finding difficulty in making connections with peers. It is profound to look back at my peculiar upbringing. In spite of personal hardships, I make the assumption that both myself and a majority of other people won't trade their current life for anyone elses.

It is humane in nature, to make good out of experiences, often regardless of good or bad.

From the third year of primary up to the 4th year of high school, I attended Renaissance College Hong Kong. Afterwards, I went to a boarding school close to the city of Trieste, in Italy.
